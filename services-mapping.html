Subsurface Mapping (by GPR + Metal Detector)

The non-invasive subsurface mapping is performed by utilizing the most recent applications of geophysics to draw a 2D or 3D images of what is underlying an innocent surface. We utilize GPR (Ground Penetration Radar) tools to prospect and receive the response of electromagnetic waves from the subsurface. Any changes or inconsistency within decay depths of waves will be mirrored back and received by the receiver at the ground surface. These received responses will help us to map the subsurface features which could be either manmade or natural. The subsurface features can be as different as sedimental soil layers, archeological elements, compacted fills, underground cavities and karst formations, underground utilities, underground storage tanks, etc.

Subsurface Utility Locating/Mapping

An application of non-invasive subsurface mapping is Subsurface Utility Mapping (SUM) which is part of Subsurface Utility Engineering (SUE) scope of work. In NES we provide SUM services which will help developers, excavators, drillers, engineers and other team members to have a better understanding of what they should expect to see at their sites. For the purpose of utility mapping, we review publicly available data, the One-Call ticket information and review the site features and anything that can give us a clue that how and where the utilities run across the site. Then with utilizing GPR and Metal Detector equipment we sweep the areas of the site as requested by the client and mark the location of the underlying utilities detected in our field program in real time. Aerial Imaging

NES provides aerial imaging services by utilizing drone technology to record high-resolution videos and capture high-quality pictures. Our recorded materials can document the actual site conditions, utilized construction methods, project process and to demonstrate the final construction product. We can provide the client either the raw recorded materials or edited video for the purpose of the presentation. We understand especially in remote areas or large sites visiting the entire site by the design team may not be possible and this service apart from documenting the condition of the site will serve as the eyes of engineers to accustom their design to the actual conditions at their sites.
