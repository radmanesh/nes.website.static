Non-Invasive Subsurface Mapping

Non-invasive subsurface mapping is utilizing the most recent applications of geophysics to draw a 2D or 3D images of what is underlying an innocent surface. We utilize GPR (ground penetration Radar) tools to summit and receive the response of electromagnetic waves to the subsurface. Any changes or inconsistency within decay depths of waves will be mirrored back and received by the receiver at ground which will help us to map the subsurface features which could be either manmade or natural such as archeological site, compacted fills, underground cavities and karst formations, underground utilities, underground storage tanks, etc.

An application of non-invasive subsurface mapping is Subsurface Utility Mapping (SUM) which is part of Subsurface Utility Engineering (SUE). The utility mapping will help developers, excavators, drillers, engineers and other team members to have a better understanding what they should expect to see at their sites. For the purpose of utility mapping, we review publicly available data and maps and the One-Call ticket information and review the site features and anything that can give us a clue that how and where the utilities run across a site. Then with utilizing GPR and Metal Detector equipment we sweep the areas of the site requested by the client and mark the location of the underlying utilities detected in our field program.
